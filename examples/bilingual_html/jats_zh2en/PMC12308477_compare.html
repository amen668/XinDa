源（中文）

医药与个人护理用品（PPCPs）作为与人类生活密切相关的新污染物，受到国内外广泛关注。PPCPs主要包括用于人类和动物疾病治疗或预防的药物以及用于个人护理品中的消毒剂、防腐剂、防晒剂等。其中，非甾体类消炎药（non-steroid anti-inflammatory drug，NSAIDs）是一类常用于治疗关节炎、免疫系统疾病和发热疼痛等的药物，因其显著的消炎、镇痛作用而被广泛应用于临床；研究显示，全球每天有超过3 000万人服用NSAIDs［1］。对羟基苯甲酸酯类可抑制微生物活动［2］，是一类广泛用于药物和个人护理产品中的防腐剂［3］。

消炎药和防腐剂因使用量大，被持续排放到环境中，造成其在包括美国旧金山湾［4］、沙特阿拉伯红海［5］、中国厦门湾［6］和渤海［7］在内的国内外海水中广泛检出。研究发现，不同种类消炎药和防腐剂的检出频率具有较大差异，例如，渤海水体中安替比林（PHZ）、异丙安替比林（PPZ）、布洛芬（IBU）和酮洛芬（KPF）的检出频率均为100%，而萘普生（NPX）、吲哚美辛（IND）、非诺洛芬（FPF）的检出频率为0~43.5%［7］。与传统持久性有机污染物相比，消炎药和防腐剂具有相对较短的半衰期，在海水中易被光降解和生物降解［8］。考虑到污染物降解受环境条件影响，季节和空间变化引起的环境条件改变、不同物质的消费模式变化会导致PPCPs分布特征发生改变。此外，随着医疗卫生条件的改善，药品消费量增大，也改变了PPCPs的消费模式和数量［9］，进而导致水环境中PPCPs的赋存和时空分布存在区域特异性。值得注意的是，虽然PPCPs在海水中的质量浓度通常为几ng/L到几十ng/L，但低浓度持续暴露会对水生生物产生不同程度的生态毒性效应，甚至危害其生命。因此，探究不同海域中PPCPs的赋存特征，评估其对水生生物的生态风险，对于揭示PPCPs污染过程和环境行为具有重要意义。

海洋沉积物是各类污染物的重要储存库，对海洋环境中污染物分布、迁移和归趋具有重要影响。海水中的部分PPCPs会经水动力扩散或随悬浮颗粒沉降进入沉积物，沉积物受底层水冲刷后，沉积物-水界面的PPCPs再悬浮至上覆水中，影响水体PPCPs分布和垂向迁移，进而导致PPCPs的赋存特征呈现时空变异性。然而，目前国内外对于海洋沉积物中PPCPs赋存特征的研究较为匮乏，对于PPCPs在沉积物-水界面的分配行为更是鲜少报道。因此，为深入了解PPCPs在环境中的归趋，亟待探究海洋环境中PPCPs的多介质分配行为。

近海蕴藏丰富的海洋资源，也是各种污染物的最终汇集地，生态环境健康面临巨大威胁。烟台和威海海域（以下简称烟威海域）位于环渤海经济区内，区域内人口密度高，旅游资源丰富。高强度的人类活动可能导致烟威海域非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂的污染状况较其他区域更为严重。以往对该地区的研究大多集中于重金属［10，11］和海洋生物［12，13］，对该地区海水和沉积物中非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂时空分布特征、多介质分配行为及生态风险的系统研究尚未深入开展，且该海域是否存在需重点关注的高风险物质尚不明确。因此，本研究以黄渤海近岸烟威海域为研究区，于不同季节采集表层海水、底层海水和沉积物样品，对海水和沉积物中15种非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂的浓度水平和分布特征进行深入探究，阐明目标污染物在沉积物-水之间的分配行为，并系统评估PPCPs混合物长期存在对不同营养水平水生生物的生态风险，识别具有高风险的优先控制污染物。研究结果可丰富近海PPCPs环境行为理论体系，为新污染物管控的国家需求提供科学依据。

1 实验部分

1.1 研究区概况和样品收集

研究区位于山东半岛北部黄海与渤海交界处烟威海域，属于温带季风气候。根据沿岸主要城市分布情况，本研究共设置4个采样断面，共14个采样点。其中A1～A4位于龙口与莱州交界海域，B1～B3位于蓬莱海域，C1～C4位于烟台海域，D1～D3位于威海海域（图1）。

本研究分别于2022年5月（春季）、2022年7月（夏季）和2023年2月（冬季）使用CTD采水器在14个采样点位采集表层海水（海平面下约0.5 m）和底层海水（海底上方1 m），共采集海水样品83个（受采样现场实际环境条件影响，冬季未在D3点位采集底层海水）。此外，在春季使用箱式采泥器于9个点位采集表层沉积物（0~2 cm）样品（图1）。水样采集后放置于清洗好的棕色玻璃瓶中，经过滤（GF/F，0.7 µm，Whatman，USA）和盐酸（0.1 mol/L）酸化（pH 2~3）后于4 ℃下黑暗环境中运输到实验室并尽快进行后续处理。沉积物样品经采集后用铝箔包装，密封后于-18℃冷冻条件下储存。采样现场同步监测水样盐度、电导率和pH值。

1.3 样品预处理和目标PPCPs分析测定

样品经预处理流程后，采用超高效液相色谱-三重四极杆质谱仪测定样品中目标PPCPs含量［8］。具体步骤如下：海水样品（1 L）经过滤和酸化后，加入500 mg Na4EDTA和30 ng同位素标准品。随后用已活化（20 mL甲醇、6 mL超纯水和6 mL pH为2~3的酸化水）的HLB固相萃取柱提取PPCPs，并先后用12 mL甲醇和6 mL甲醇-丙酮（50∶50，v/v）混合液进行洗脱。洗脱液经氮吹至近干，并用甲醇定容至1.0 mL后待测。沉积物经冷冻干燥、研磨过筛后，取2 g置于50 mL离心管中，加入30 ng同位素标准品。之后加入10 mL EDTA-McΙlvaine缓冲液（pH 4.0，采用柠檬酸、磷酸氢二钠和乙二胺四乙酸二钠配制），涡旋1 min，再加10 mL乙腈，涡旋1 min，超声30 min。加入4 g硫酸镁和1 g氯化钠，立即强力振摇3 min，离心，取7 mL上清液转移至棕瓶，氮吹至近干，并用甲醇定容至1.0 mL后待测。

1.4 质量保证和质量控制

本研究遵循严格的质量控制和保证程序。为保证实验数据的可靠性，每个批次设置一个过程空白、一个空白加标、一个基质加标和一个平行样品，且每个样品在提取前均加入同位素标准品指示回收率。为核查仪器稳定性，每20个样品插入一个已知浓度标准样品，并将已知浓度标准样品的含量偏差控制在20%的范围内。目标物质的同位素标准品回收率在50%~120%内，且相对标准偏差小于15%。此外，分别以3倍和10倍信噪比确定目标物质的方法检出限（MDL）和方法定量限（MQL）［7］，海水和沉积物样品中目标物质的MDL范围分别为0.03~2.00 ng/L和0.02~0.85 ng/g，目标物质的MQL范围分别为0.20~2.23 ng/L和0.12~1.63 ng/g。

1.5 生态风险评估及优先控制PPCPs识别

自然水体中的PPCPs通常以混合物的形式存在，它们在水生环境中的风险不会单独发生，忽视混合物风险可能会低估对水生生物的实际影响［14］。为评估多种PPCPs残留造成的生态风险，本研究基于浓度叠加模型［15］评估了PPCPs混合物对于藻类、甲壳类和鱼类的总风险熵值RQT（total risk quotients）：

RQT=∑i=1nRQi=∑i=1nMECiPNECiRQ_{T} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}{RQ_{i}} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\frac{MEC_{i}}{PNEC_{i}}

其中，MEC i （measured environmental concentration of compound i）为化合物i在海水中的实测浓度，PNEC i （predicted no-effect concentration）为化合物i的预测无效应浓度，RQ i 为化合物i的生态风险熵值。风险熵值越高，表明其生态风险等级越高。根据风险熵值的大小，将生态风险等级分为高风险（RQT≥1）、中风险（0.1≤RQT<1）和低风险（RQT<0.1）［8，15-17］。

目标PPCPs预测无效应浓度的计算是基于以生长或繁殖异常为终点（endpoints）的慢性毒性数据——无观察效应浓度（no observed effect concentration， NOEC）或最低观察效应浓度（lowest observed effect concentration， LOEC）和相应评估因子（assessment factor， AF）［8，14］，计算公式如下：

PNEC=NOECorLOECAFPNEC = \frac{NOEC\ or\ LOEC\ }{AF}\

其中，目标PPCPs对藻类、甲壳类和鱼类的NOECNOEC/LOEC值收集自美国环境保护局ECOSAR v2.2软件。当3个营养级生物均有可用慢性毒性数据时，AF值取10［8］；此外，当同一营养级生物有多个可用毒性数据时，选择可用毒性数据的最低值计算PNEC，以实现对最敏感物种的最大程度保护。收集得到目标PPCPs对不同水生生物的慢性毒性数据和计算得到的预测无效应浓度见表3。

1.6 统计分析

本研究主要采用SPSS 27软件进行数据检验，在显著性水平0.05下检验统计学显著性和Spearman相关性。采用Origin 2022绘制箱线图，采用ArcGIS软件（v10.8）分析非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂的空间分布特征。当物质浓度小于方法检出限时，使用Excel中的任意赋值功能（RANDBETWEEN）将物质浓度赋值为0~LOD之间的任意数值以进行数据统计分析［18］。

2 结果与讨论

2.1 海水和沉积物中目标PPCPs的检出频率和浓度水平

目标PPCPs的检出频率和浓度水平如图2所示。除扑热息痛（PTL）和PPZ外，其余13种PPCPs在海水和沉积物中均被检出（图2a）。其中，PHZ、FPF、对羟基苯甲酸甲酯（MPB）、对羟基苯甲酸乙酯（EPB）和对羟基苯甲酸丙酯（PPB）在海水中的检出频率为70%~100%；IBU、KPF、MPB、EPB、PPB在沉积物中的检出频率均高于50%。整体上，PPCPs的检出频率与之前临近区域的检出频率相当或略低［8，15］，表明目标PPCP在研究区普遍存在。

海水中PPCPs总质量浓度范围为2.31~662.31 ng/L，PHZ、FPF和MPB是海水中主要的PPCPs（图2b、2c）。整体上，烟威海域采样点海水中PPCPs的浓度平均值低于或与世界其他区域海水中目标物的浓度水平相当［5，19-21］。前期研究表明PHZ是渤海海域主要的PPCPs污染物，PHZ、FPF和MPB对总PPCPs浓度的贡献率为55%~92%［7］。本研究中，MPB分别占春季底层水和夏季表层水中PPCPs总浓度的69%和67%（图2c），这可能与其在我国市场上占据主导地位有关［3］。然而，以往关于烟威海域的研究中未涉及MPB［8，22，23］，因此可能低估了MPB所导致的防腐剂污染。PHZ在世界范围内均已被检测到［8，24-26］，本研究中PHZ的质量浓度（平均值：3.27 ng/L）与烟台湾（平均值：3.40 ng/L）［8］、德国-荷兰跨界Vecht河（平均值：2.09×10-2 ng/L）相当，低于荷兰莱茵河（平均值：135 ng/L）［26］、东地中海沿岸（西班牙）米哈雷斯河（最大值：1 984 ng/L）［27］；FPF的质量浓度（平均值：3.81 ng/L）高于烟台湾（最大值：0.09 ng/L）［8］；MPB的质量浓度（平均值：30.16 ng/L）低于巴西（最大值：980 ng/L）［28］，高于渤海（平均值：3.3 ng/L）［7］。

沉积物中PPCPs的总含量范围为57.29~126.34 ng/g（图2d）。不同于海水，沉积物中非甾体类消炎药的浓度整体高于防腐剂（图2d），值得注意的是，海水中的主导物质PHZ在沉积物中的浓度低于其检出限（图2d），可能与其在水中较高的溶解度有关；相反地，IBU和KPF在海水中的浓度低于其检出限，但在沉积物中具有较高浓度，这可能与这两种物质的高log Kow值（分别为3.72和2.81）有关。特别地，KPF是沉积物中浓度最高的污染物。本研究中IBU含量（平均值：12.31 ng/g）甚至高于北运河沉积物（平均值：ND~0.46 ng/g）［29］，但低于地中海沿岸湿地沉积物中的IBU含量（最大值：100 ng/g）［30］；长江口沉积物中检测到了IND（含量范围：12~164 ng/g）［31］，但本研究区域沉积物中并未检测到IND；本研究区域MPB与地中海沿岸湿地的含量相当（平均值低于10 ng/g）［30］。

2.2 海水中目标PPCPs浓度的季节变化

PPCPs浓度呈现季节性差异。非甾体类消炎药、防腐剂和PPCPs总浓度整体表现为夏季高于春季和冬季（图3）。夏季防腐剂的高浓度主要与MPB和PPB在夏季的较高浓度有关，其平均质量浓度分别为30.16 ng/L和1.45 ng/L。与其他两个季节相比，夏季消费者为维持自身卫生状况和抵御紫外线伤害，日常生活中个人洗护产品用量增大，进而导致防腐剂在夏季的排放量增加。此外，夏季沿海城市旅游人口的增长同样是导致防腐剂排放量增加的潜在因素。已有研究报道夏季丰水期时，较强的旅游活动导致防腐剂和防晒剂在九龙江入海口具有较高的浓度［32］。本研究区域夏季MPB的质量浓度要远高于夏季新西兰利特尔顿港（<2.7 ng/L）［33］和葡萄牙阿威罗地区海水（平均值：9.9 ng/L）［34］的浓度水平，而PPB浓度与上述研究区域浓度水平相当［33，34］。

主要非甾体类消炎药IBU、PHZ、FPF在夏季具有较高的浓度，平均质量浓度分别为15.15、3.27和3.81 ng/L（图3）。其中，IBU只在夏季样品中被检测到，FPF在春季和夏季样品中被检测到，PHZ在3季样品中都被检测到。IBU是一种源自丙酸的非甾体类消炎药，是最常用的非处方药之一，在我国七大流域（长江、太湖、黄河、松花江、黑龙江、大运河和东江）［35］、巴西桑托斯湾［36］、葡萄牙北部海水［37］和挪威特罗姆瑟湾［38］均已被检测到；其中，葡萄牙北部与本研究区域相似，在夏季检测到高浓度的IBU［37］。FPF也是一种广泛使用的非甾体类消炎药，可治疗类风湿性关节炎、退行性关节病、强直性脊柱炎和痛风［39］。本研究中FPF夏季浓度显著高于春季和冬季，而土耳其马尔马拉金角湾河口则是秋季浓度显著高于春季和夏季［40］，且在马尔马拉金角湾河口中FPF一年四季被检测到。本研究未涉及秋季样本可能是导致上述差异的原因，应在后续研究中对四季样品展开充分研究。PHZ是一种镇痛和解热药物，在临床上广泛用于缓解头痛、发烧和全身疼痛［41］，其被确定为烟台湾海域主要污染物［8］。PHZ在三季都被检测到可能是因为其生化持久性强，废水处理厂对PHZ的去除效率仅为33%左右［41］。本研究中夏季消炎药和总PPCPs夏季浓度显著高于其他两个季节，这与以往的研究结果不同［8，42，43］。可能是由于夏季为烟台、威海旅游旺季，大量流动人口带来了非甾体类消炎药消费量和释放量的增长［44，45］。此外，夏季采样时间在黄河调水调沙之后，因此污染物浓度还可能受黄河调水调沙的影响，这需要结合悬浮颗粒物上PPCPs浓度数据，以更好地证明调水调沙对海水中污染物浓度的影响，这是未来研究的重要方向。

2.3 海水和沉积物中目标PPCPs空间分布特征

在水平方向上，每个采样断面海水中PPCPs总浓度未呈现出明显的离岸由近及远递减的趋势。整体看来，春季和冬季渤海海域采样点（A1~A4）PPCPs总浓度略高于黄海海域采样点（B1~D3），夏季黄海海域采样点中PPCPs总浓度略高于渤海海域采样点（图4）。注入黄海南部莱州湾内的黄河是我国北方最大河流，全年入海水量高达226.50亿立方米［46］，是黄海和莱州湾内各类污染物的重要来源。研究表明，在莱州湾主要入海河流中，黄河输入对于莱州湾PPCPs的贡献最大（近90%）［47］。随着春季黄河水量的增加，其输入至莱州湾PPCPs污染物的总量增加，导致莱州湾PPCPs浓度高于黄海。渤海海域冬季会出现大量海冰［48，49］，导致PPCPs污染物扩散速度大幅度下降。同时大量海冰的存在也会减弱水体中的光照强度，进而导致PPCPs污染物光降解速率下降［8，50］，最终使渤海海域采样点冬季PPCPs的浓度变高。夏季黄海采样点PPCPs浓度高可能是因为夏季是旅游旺季，蓬莱、烟台和威海三地是山东省热门旅游城市，大量游客的到来增加了PPCPs的消费量和释放量［6］，MPB是常用的化妆品防腐剂，夏季B1点位MPB的质量浓度为638.87 ng/L可以很好地佐证这点。

在垂直方向上，虽然春季底层水PPCPs总浓度整体高于表层水，夏季表底层水PPCPs总浓度的分布格局不同，冬季表层水PPCPs总浓度整体高于底层水（图4），但3个季节表层水和底层水PPCPs总浓度并未呈现显著性差异，而且3个季节表底层水非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂的浓度也未呈现出显著性差异（p>0.05，图5）。这一结果与此前对于莱州湾海域中抗生素和个人护理品垂向变化的结果一致［15，51］。研究表明，污染物的垂向变化特征受其自身理化性质和环境条件的影响［52，53］。本研究中目标PPCPs总浓度未呈现显著性差异，可能与海水垂直交换能力强、海水垂直混匀有关［54-56］。在沉积物中，PPCPs浓度为黄海点位浓度高于渤海（图4）。需要注意的是，受研究条件限制，本研究中采样点位数量较少，可能在一定程度上限制了研究结果的代表性。未来研究应注意优化采样点数量，以进一步提高研究的全面性和代表性。

2.4 沉积物-水分配系数

污染物在海洋环境中的分布取决于很多因素，例如沉积物-水分配系数［57］、辛醇-水分配系数［57］、污染物本身性质等，沉积物-水分配系数可提供污染物在海洋环境中迁移和归宿的信息。

沉积物-水分配系数用公式（4）、（5）［58］进行计算：

Kd=Cs/Cw×1000K_{d} = C_{s}/C_{w} \times 1000

Koc=Kd×100/focK_{oc} = K_{d} \times 100/f_{oc}

其中C s和C w分别为沉积物（ng/g）和水（ng/L）中PPCPs的含量；f oc为沉积物中总有机碳含量（%）。

本研究所涉及的物质中EPB和PPB的log K oc值为（3.99±0.95） cm3/g和（3.80±0.57） cm3/g（图6），只选取这两种物质计算log K oc值的原因是其他物质未在水中检出或未在沉积物中检出，导致K d或log K oc值无法计算。以往研究很少涉及EPB、PPB的沉积物-水分配系数，通过与使用EPIWEB 4.1（US EPA）根据物质理化性质预测的log K oc值对比发现，本研究中通过野外采样实际测量的log K oc值均高于其预测值（图6a）。根据以往其他物质的研究，物质的沉积物-水分配系数受物质的性质与用量、水体化学性质和沉积物性质［59］等因素影响。在海洋环境中，盐度是影响有机化合物在沉积物-水之间分配行为的重要环境因素，盐度的增加可能导致有机化合物与沉积物间亲和力的改变［60］。本次研究中，两种物质log K oc值和海水pH、盐度及电导率均无显著相关性（p>0.05），表明实际环境中有机污染物分配行为的复杂性。未来需进一步深化此方面的研究，以全面阐明环境因素对PPCPs沉积物-水分配行为的影响。

2.5 PPCPs的联合生态风险评估及风险优先级排序

由于PPCPs在海洋环境中是混合存在的，无论毒性机制如何，混合物的毒性可能是所有单个化合物毒性作用之和［61］。因此，本研究利用公式（1）分别计算了PPCPs对藻类、甲壳类和鱼类的总风险熵值，以评估PPCPs混合物对不同营养水平水生生物的联合生态风险［8，15，16，29］。结果表明，尽管PPCPs混合物对甲壳类的风险高于藻类和鱼类，但其对3类生物均呈低风险（图7a）。这一研究结果与之前马来西亚雪兰莪州［62］和天津某水产养殖区［63］的研究结果一致，均为低风险；和河套灌区［64］的研究结果不一致，河套灌区的研究结果为PPCP物质对生物呈现中高风险，少部分物质呈现低风险，这可能与PPCPs污染物类型和毒性数据选取的差异有关［15］。此外，渤海海域采样点中PPCPs混合物对藻类、甲壳类动物和鱼类的生态风险熵均值均高于黄海海域，但两海域间未呈现出显著性差异（p>0.05，图7a）。

为对研究区海水中目标PPCPs进行风险优先级排序［65］，本研究采用优化的风险熵值法（公式（3））计算了单一PPCP物质（检出率高于50%）对其最敏感物种的优先级指数PI（图7b）。结果表明，所有检出频率大于50%的物质的优先级指数PI均为0，均被归类为安全物质。尽管本研究中非甾体类消炎药和防腐剂的混合物对3类生物的风险均较低，但是对于PPCPs的代谢产物并没有深入研究，同时以往研究［66］认为羟基化布洛芬代谢物如1-羟基布洛芬、2-羟基布洛芬、1，2-二羟基布洛芬比母体布洛芬具有更高的毒性，未来仍需对PPCPs及其代谢产物进行持续监测以评估其生态风险。此外，以往该地区的相关研究缺乏对MPB的探讨，因此在后续污染物治理中应着重关注该物质。

3 结论

本研究在山东半岛烟威海域探究了两类共15种常用PPCPs的时空分布、沉积物-水分配系数、生态风险及污染物优先级，分别有13种和5种PPCPs在海水和沉积物中被检出，PPCPs在水体和沉积物中的含量分别为2.31~662.31 ng/L和57.29~126.34 ng/g。PPCPs的浓度变化不仅与季节性消费模式有关，也跟污染物自身性质、环境条件的影响有关。在空间变化中，垂直方向上3个季节表层水和底层水并未展现显著性差异，水平方向上春季和冬季渤海海域采样点海水中PPCPs浓度要高于黄海海域采样点，夏季黄海海域采样点PPCPs浓度高于渤海海域采样点，可能与环境条件差异和PPCPs消费模式的变化有关。此外，环境因素可以影响PPCPs的沉积物-水分配系数，进而影响其环境归趋。生态风险方面，各类PPCPs对藻类、甲壳类和鱼类均呈低风险，9种PPCPs优先级指数均为0，但是MPB在3个点位的风险熵值高于其他物质和其他点位，所以在未来的研究中，有必要对该区域的MPB污染物着重关注，并研究其去除方式。

译（English）

Pharmaceuticals and personal care products (PPCPs), as emerging contaminants closely linked to human life, have attracted widespread domestic and international attention. PPCPs primarily include therapeutic or prophylactic drugs used for humans and animals, as well as disinfectants, preservatives, and UV filters employed in personal care products. Among them, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) are widely used clinically to treat arthritis, autoimmune disorders, and fever-related pain due to their potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects; studies indicate that over 30 million people worldwide consume NSAIDs daily [1]. Parabens inhibit microbial activity [2] and are widely applied as preservatives in pharmaceuticals and personal care products [3].

NSAIDs and preservatives—due to their large-scale usage and continuous discharge into the environment—are widely detected in coastal waters globally, including San Francisco Bay (USA) [4], the Red Sea (Saudi Arabia) [5], Xiamen Bay (China) [6], and the Bohai Sea (China) [7]. Studies reveal substantial inter-compound variation in detection frequencies: for instance, in the Bohai Sea, antipyrine (PHZ), propyphenazone (PPZ), ibuprofen (IBU), and ketoprofen (KPF) were detected at 100% frequency, whereas naproxen (NPX), indomethacin (IND), and fenoprofen (FPF) exhibited detection frequencies ranging from 0% to 43.5% [7]. Compared with conventional persistent organic pollutants (POPs), NSAIDs and preservatives possess relatively short half-lives and undergo rapid photolytic and biotic degradation in seawater [8]. Given that degradation kinetics are environmentally sensitive, seasonal and spatial variations in environmental conditions—as well as shifts in consumption patterns across compounds—can significantly alter PPCP distribution characteristics. Moreover, improvements in healthcare infrastructure have increased pharmaceutical consumption, thereby modifying both the quantity and pattern of PPCP use [9], leading to region-specific occurrence and spatiotemporal distribution of PPCPs in aquatic environments. Notably, although aqueous PPCP concentrations typically range from several to tens of ng/L, chronic low-level exposure can induce varying degrees of ecotoxicological effects on aquatic organisms—even threatening their survival. Therefore, elucidating the occurrence characteristics of PPCPs across different marine regions and assessing their ecological risks to aquatic biota is critical for understanding PPCP contamination processes and environmental behavior.

Marine sediments serve as critical reservoirs for diverse contaminants and exert profound influences on the distribution, transport, and ultimate fate of pollutants in marine environments. A fraction of PPCPs in seawater enters sediments via hydrodynamic diffusion or co-sedimentation with suspended particles; subsequently, resuspension of sediment-associated PPCPs at the sediment–water interface—driven by benthic current scouring—reintroduces them into overlying water, thereby modulating aqueous PPCP distribution and vertical migration and ultimately contributing to spatiotemporal variability in PPCP occurrence. However, research on PPCP occurrence characteristics in marine sediments remains scarce both domestically and internationally, and reports on sediment–water partitioning behavior of PPCPs are exceedingly rare. Thus, to deepen understanding of PPCP environmental fate, urgent investigation into multi-media partitioning behavior of PPCPs in marine environments is warranted.

Coastal zones harbor abundant marine resources yet also function as final sinks for diverse pollutants, placing ecosystem health under severe threat. The Yantai–Weihai coastal zone (hereinafter referred to as the Yan–Wei coastal zone), located at the junction of the Yellow Sea and Bohai Sea on the northern Shandong Peninsula, lies within the Bohai Rim Economic Zone and features high population density and rich tourism resources. Intensive anthropogenic activities may render the Yan–Wei coastal zone more severely contaminated by NSAIDs and preservatives than other regions. Previous studies in this area have predominantly focused on heavy metals [10, 11] and marine biota [12, 13]; systematic investigations into the spatiotemporal distribution, multi-media partitioning behavior, and ecological risks of NSAIDs and preservatives in seawater and sediments remain insufficient. Furthermore, it remains unclear whether high-risk priority-contaminants exist in this region. Accordingly, this study selected the Yan–Wei coastal zone at the Yellow Sea–Bohai Sea interface as the study area and collected surface seawater, bottom seawater, and sediment samples across multiple seasons. It comprehensively investigated the concentration levels and distribution patterns of 15 NSAIDs and preservatives in seawater and sediments, clarified the sediment–water partitioning behavior of target pollutants, systematically assessed the long-term ecological risks posed by PPCP mixtures to aquatic organisms across trophic levels, and identified high-risk priority-control pollutants. The findings will enrich the theoretical framework of PPCP environmental behavior in coastal zones and provide scientific support for national regulatory initiatives targeting emerging contaminants.

1 Experimental Section

1.1 Study Area Overview and Sample Collection

The study area is located at the junction of the Yellow Sea and Bohai Sea in the northern Shandong Peninsula—the Yan–Wei coastal zone—and exhibits a temperate monsoon climate. Based on the distribution of major coastal cities, four sampling transects comprising 14 sampling sites were established: A1–A4 in the Longkou–Laizhou boundary waters, B1–B3 in the Penglai waters, C1–C4 in the Yantai waters, and D1–D3 in the Weihai waters [图1].

Seawater samples (surface: ~0.5 m below sea surface; bottom: 1 m above seabed) were collected at the 14 sampling sites in May 2022 (spring), July 2022 (summer), and February 2023 (winter) using a CTD water sampler, yielding a total of 83 seawater samples (bottom seawater was not collected at site D3 during winter due to field constraints). Additionally, surface sediments (0–2 cm depth) were collected at nine sites during spring using an Ekman grab sampler [图1]. After collection, seawater samples were stored in pre-cleaned amber glass bottles, filtered through GF/F filters (0.7 µm, Whatman, USA), acidified with 0.1 mol/L HCl to pH 2–3, and transported to the laboratory in darkness at 4 °C for prompt subsequent processing. Sediment samples were wrapped in aluminum foil, sealed, and stored frozen at −18 °C. Concurrently, salinity, conductivity, and pH of seawater were measured in situ.

Ultra-high-performance liquid chromatography–triple quadrupole mass spectrometry (UHPLC–MS/MS; Applied Biosystems, Framingham, USA); CTD water sampler and Ekman grab sampler (KC-Denmark, Denmark); HLB solid-phase extraction (SPE) cartridges (500 mg, 6 mL; Waters, USA).

1.3 Sample Pretreatment and Analytical Determination of Target PPCPs

After pretreatment, target PPCPs in samples were quantified using ultra-high-performance liquid chromatography coupled with triple-quadrupole mass spectrometry (UHPLC-MS/MS) 8. Specific procedures are as follows: For seawater samples (1 L), filtration and acidification were performed first, followed by addition of 500 mg Na₄EDTA and 30 ng of isotopically labeled internal standards. PPCPs were then extracted using an activated hydrophilic-lipophilic balanced (HLB) solid-phase extraction (SPE) cartridge (activated with 20 mL methanol, 6 mL ultrapure water, and 6 mL acidified water at pH 2–3). Elution was carried out sequentially with 12 mL methanol and 6 mL methanol-acetone (50:50, v/v). The eluate was concentrated to near dryness under a gentle nitrogen stream and reconstituted in 1.0 mL methanol for analysis. For sediment samples, freeze-drying, grinding, and sieving (<2 mm) were conducted prior to weighing 2 g into a 50-mL centrifuge tube; 30 ng of isotopically labeled internal standards were added. Subsequently, 10 mL EDTA-McIlvaine buffer (pH 4.0, prepared from citric acid, disodium hydrogen phosphate, and disodium ethylenediaminetetraacetate) was added, and the mixture was vortexed for 1 min. Then, 10 mL acetonitrile was added, and the mixture was vortexed for 1 min and sonicated for 30 min. Next, 4 g anhydrous magnesium sulfate and 1 g sodium chloride were added, followed immediately by vigorous shaking for 3 min and centrifugation. A 7-mL aliquot of the supernatant was transferred to an amber vial, concentrated to near dryness under nitrogen, and reconstituted in 1.0 mL methanol for analysis.

1.4 Quality Assurance and Quality Control

This study adhered to rigorous quality assurance and quality control (QA/QC) procedures. To ensure data reliability, each analytical batch included one procedural blank, one blank spike, one matrix spike, and one duplicate sample; moreover, isotopically labeled internal standards were spiked into each sample prior to extraction to monitor recovery efficiency. To verify instrumental stability, a standard reference sample of known concentration was analyzed every 20 samples, and the deviation of its measured concentration from the nominal value was maintained within ±20%. Recoveries of isotopically labeled internal standards for target compounds ranged from 50% to 120%, with relative standard deviations (RSDs) <15%. Furthermore, method detection limits (MDLs) and method quantification limits (MQLs) for target compounds were determined at signal-to-noise ratios (S/N) of 3 and 10, respectively [7]. The MDLs for seawater and sediment samples ranged from 0.03 to 2.00 ng/L and from 0.02 to 0.85 ng/g, respectively; the corresponding MQLs ranged from 0.20 to 2.23 ng/L and from 0.12 to 1.63 ng/g, respectively.

1.5 Ecological Risk Assessment and Identification of Priority-Control PPCPs

Pharmaceuticals and personal care products (PPCPs) in natural aquatic systems typically occur as complex mixtures; their ecological risks in aquatic environments are not independent but rather cumulative. Ignoring mixture effects may lead to underestimation of actual impacts on aquatic biota [14]. To assess the combined ecological risk posed by multiple co-occurring PPCPs, this study employed a concentration addition model [15] to calculate the total risk quotient (RQT) for algal, crustacean, and fish communities:

RQT=∑i=1nRQi=∑i=1nMECiPNECiRQ_{T} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}{RQ_{i}} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n}\frac{MEC_{i}}{PNEC_{i}}

where MEC i (measured environmental concentration of compound i) denotes the measured concentration of compound i in seawater, PNEC i (predicted no-effect concentration) denotes the predicted no-effect concentration for compound i, and RQ i represents the individual ecological risk quotient for compound i. A higher RQ value indicates a greater ecological risk level. Based on the magnitude of RQT, ecological risk levels were categorized as high risk (RQT ≥ 1), medium risk (0.1 ≤ RQT < 1), and low risk (RQT < 0.1) [8, 15–17].

The calculation of predicted no-effect concentrations (PNECs) for target PPCPs was based on chronic toxicity endpoints—specifically, no observed effect concentrations (NOECs) or lowest observed effect concentrations (LOECs)—derived from studies reporting growth or reproductive impairment, together with appropriate assessment factors (AFs) [8, 14]. The calculation formula is as follows:

PNEC=NOECorLOECAFPNEC = \frac{NOEC\ or\ LOEC\ }{AF}\

Chronic NOEC/LOEC values for target PPCPs toward algae, crustaceans, and fish were obtained from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s ECOSAR v2.2 software. When chronic toxicity data were available for all three trophic levels, an AF of 10 was applied [8]. Additionally, when multiple chronic toxicity data points were available for the same trophic level, the lowest reported value was selected to compute the PNEC, thereby ensuring maximum protection for the most sensitive species. Moreover, to effectively identify priority-control PPCPs with high ecological risk, this study calculated the priority index (PI) for each compound based on an optimized risk quotient method [8, 9, 15, 17]:

PIi=RQMi×(N1N×100%)PI_{i} = RQ_{Mi} \times \left( {\frac{N_{1}}{N} \times 100\%} \right)

where RQM i (mean risk quotient value of compound i) denotes the mean risk quotient of compound i for its most sensitive species—i.e., calculated using the lowest predicted no-effect concentration (PNEC) among the three trophic levels (algae, crustaceans, and fish); N 1 is the number of samples in which the concentration exceeds PNEC i , and N is the total number of samples (N = 83). Based on the PI value, target compounds are classified as high-risk (PI ≥ 1), medium-risk (0.1 ≤ PI < 1), low-risk (0.01 ≤ PI < 0.1), negligible-risk (0 < PI < 0.01), or safe (PI = 0) [8, 15, 17].

2 Results and discussion

2.1 Detection frequencies and concentration levels of target PPCPs in seawater and sediment

Detection frequencies and concentration levels of the target PPCPs are presented in 图2. Except for paracetamol (PTL) and phenazone (PPZ), all other 13 PPCPs were detected in both seawater and sediment [图2a]. Specifically, phenazone (PHZ), flufenamic acid (FPF), methylparaben (MPB), ethylparaben (EPB), and propylparaben (PPB) exhibited detection frequencies of 70–100% in seawater; ibuprofen (IBU), ketoprofen (KPF), MPB, EPB, and PPB showed detection frequencies exceeding 50% in sediment. Overall, the detection frequencies of PPCPs in this study are comparable to or slightly lower than those reported previously for adjacent regions [8, 15], indicating widespread occurrence of the target PPCPs in the study area.

The total mass concentrations of PPCPs in seawater ranged from 2.31 to 662.31 ng/L. PHZ, FPF, and MPB were the dominant PPCPs in seawater (Figs. 2b, 2c). Overall, the average PPCP concentrations at sampling sites in the Yantai–Weihai coastal zone were lower than or comparable to those reported elsewhere globally [5, 19–21]. Previous studies identified PHZ as the predominant PPCP contaminant in the Bohai Sea, with PHZ, FPF, and MPB collectively contributing 55–92% to the total PPCP concentration [7]. In this study, MPB accounted for 69% and 67% of the total PPCP concentration in spring bottom water and summer surface water, respectively [图2c], likely attributable to its dominant market share in China [3]. However, MPB was not investigated in prior studies of the Yantai–Weihai region [8, 22, 23], potentially leading to underestimation of preservative pollution attributable to MPB. PHZ has been widely detected worldwide [8, 24–26]; its measured mass concentration in this study (mean: 3.27 ng/L) is comparable to that in Yantai–Taiwan (mean: 3.40 ng/L) [8] and the transboundary Vecht River (Germany–Netherlands; mean: 2.09 × 10⁻² ng/L), but lower than those in the Rhine River (Netherlands; mean: 135 ng/L) [26] and the Mijares River (Spain, Eastern Mediterranean coast; maximum: 1,984 ng/L) [27]. The mass concentration of FPF (mean: 3.81 ng/L) exceeded the maximum value reported for Yantai–Taiwan (0.09 ng/L) [8]; the mass concentration of MPB (mean: 30.16 ng/L) was lower than the maximum value reported for Brazil (980 ng/L) [28], but higher than the mean value reported for the Bohai Sea (3.3 ng/L) [7].

The total content of PPCPs in sediment ranged from 57.29 to 126.34 ng/g [图2d]. Unlike seawater, NSAIDs exhibited higher overall concentrations than preservatives in sediment [图2d]. Notably, PHZ—the dominant compound in seawater—was below its detection limit in sediment [图2d], possibly due to its relatively high aqueous solubility. Conversely, IBU and KPF were below their detection limits in seawater but exhibited relatively high concentrations in sediment, likely attributable to their high log K{{NL}}ow values (3.72 and 2.81, respectively). Notably, KPF was the most abundant contaminant in sediment. The mean IBU content in this study (12.31 ng/g) exceeded that in sediment from the North Canal (ND–0.46 ng/g) [29], yet remained lower than the maximum value reported for wetland sediments along the Mediterranean coast (100 ng/g) [30]. Indomethacin (IND) was detected in sediment from the Yangtze Estuary (range: 12–164 ng/g) [31], but was not detected in sediment from the present study area. The MPB content in the study area was comparable to that in Mediterranean coastal wetlands (mean < 10 ng/g) [30].

2.2 Seasonal variation of target PPCP concentrations in seawater

PPCP concentrations exhibited seasonal variation. Overall, concentrations of NSAIDs, preservatives, and total PPCPs were higher in summer than in spring and winter [图3]. The elevated summer concentrations of preservatives were primarily driven by higher concentrations of MPB and PPB, with mean mass concentrations of 30.16 ng/L and 1.45 ng/L, respectively. Compared with the other two seasons, increased usage of personal care products during summer—driven by heightened consumer demand for hygiene maintenance and ultraviolet radiation protection—likely contributed to greater preservative discharge. Additionally, increased tourism-related population influx into coastal cities during summer represents another potential factor elevating preservative emissions. Prior studies have reported elevated concentrations of preservatives and UV filters at the Jiulong River estuary during the summer flood season, attributed to intensified tourism activity [32]. In this study, the summer MPB concentration markedly exceeded those reported for Lyttelton Harbour (New Zealand; <2.7 ng/L) [33] and the Aveiro region (Portugal; mean: 9.9 ng/L) [34], whereas the PPB concentration was comparable to those reported for the aforementioned regions [33, 34].

Major NSAIDs—including IBU, PHZ, and FPF—exhibited higher concentrations in summer, with mean mass concentrations of 15.15, 3.27, and 3.81 ng/L, respectively [图3]. IBU was detected exclusively in summer samples; FPF was detected in both spring and summer samples; and PHZ was detected across all three seasons. IBU, a propionic-acid-derived NSAID, is one of the most commonly used over-the-counter drugs and has been detected in China’s seven major river basins (Yangtze, Taihu, Yellow, Songhua, Heilongjiang, Grand Canal, and Dongjiang) [35], Santos Bay (Brazil) [36], northern Portuguese seawater [37], and Tromsøfjorden (Norway) [38]. Notably, northern Portugal—similar to the present study region—reported high summer IBU concentrations [37]. FPF is a widely used NSAID indicated for rheumatoid arthritis, degenerative joint disease, ankylosing spondylitis, and gout [39]. In this study, summer FPF concentrations were significantly higher than those in spring and winter, whereas in the Marmara Golden Horn estuary (Turkey), autumn concentrations significantly exceeded those in spring and summer [40], and FPF was detected year-round. The absence of autumn sampling in this study may account for this discrepancy, warranting comprehensive four-season sampling in future investigations. PHZ, a clinically prevalent analgesic and antipyretic used to alleviate headache, fever, and generalized pain [41], has been identified as the primary contaminant in the Yantai–Taiwan sea area [8]. Its consistent detection across all three seasons likely reflects its strong biochemical persistence and the low removal efficiency (~33%) achieved by wastewater treatment plants [41]. In this study, summer concentrations of NSAIDs and total PPCPs were significantly higher than those in the other two seasons—a finding inconsistent with previous reports [8, 42, 43]. This discrepancy may stem from the summer tourism peak in Yantai and Weihai, where large transient populations drive increased consumption and release of NSAIDs [44, 45]. Furthermore, summer sampling occurred post–Yellow River water-sediment regulation, suggesting that pollutant concentrations may also be influenced by this operation. To rigorously assess this influence, future work should integrate suspended particulate matter–associated PPCP concentration data—a key direction for subsequent research.

2.3 Spatial distribution characteristics of target PPCPs in seawater and sediment

Horizontally, no clear offshore decreasing trend in total PPCP concentrations was observed across sampling transects in seawater. Overall, total PPCP concentrations at Bohai Sea sampling sites (A1–A4) were slightly higher than those at Yellow Sea sites (B1–D3) in spring and winter, whereas Yellow Sea sites exhibited marginally higher concentrations in summer [图4]. The Yellow River—the largest northern Chinese river discharging into the southern Laizhou Bay—contributes an annual freshwater volume of 22.65 billion m³ [46], serving as a major source of diverse pollutants to the Yellow Sea and Laizhou Bay. Studies indicate that the Yellow River contributes ~90% of the total PPCP load entering Laizhou Bay via its principal inflowing rivers [47]. Increased Yellow River flow during spring enhances the total PPCP load delivered to Laizhou Bay, resulting in higher PPCP concentrations there relative to the broader Yellow Sea. Extensive sea ice formation occurs in the Bohai Sea during winter [48, 49], drastically reducing the diffusion rate of PPCP contaminants. Concurrently, sea ice attenuates light penetration, thereby suppressing the photodegradation rate of PPCPs [8, 50], ultimately elevating winter PPCP concentrations at Bohai Sea sampling sites. Elevated summer PPCP concentrations at Yellow Sea sites may reflect the summer tourism peak: Penglai, Yantai, and Weihai are popular tourist destinations in Shandong Province, and the influx of large numbers of tourists increases PPCP consumption and discharge [6]. MPB—a common cosmetic preservative—reached a mass concentration of 638.87 ng/L at site B1 in summer, strongly supporting this interpretation.

Vertically, although total PPCP concentrations in spring bottom water were generally higher than those in surface water, the vertical distribution pattern differed in summer, and total PPCP concentrations in winter surface water were generally higher than those in bottom water [图4]. Nevertheless, no statistically significant differences were observed between surface and bottom water concentrations across any season (p > 0.05), nor were significant differences found for NSAID or preservative concentrations between surface and bottom waters across seasons (p > 0.05) [图5]. This finding aligns with prior observations of vertical profiles of antibiotics and personal care products in Laizhou Bay [15, 51]. Vertical distribution patterns of contaminants are governed by intrinsic physicochemical properties and ambient environmental conditions [52, 53]. The absence of significant vertical concentration gradients in this study may reflect strong vertical mixing and exchange capacity in seawater [54–56]. In sediment, PPCP concentrations were higher at Yellow Sea sites than at Bohai Sea sites [图4]. It should be noted that the limited number of sampling sites—constrained by logistical considerations—may partially compromise the representativeness of the findings. Future studies should optimize sampling site density to enhance comprehensiveness and representativeness.

2.4 Sediment–water partition coefficients

The distribution of contaminants in marine environments depends on multiple factors—including sediment–water partition coefficients [57], octanol–water partition coefficients [57], and intrinsic contaminant properties—where sediment–water partition coefficients provide critical information regarding contaminant transport and fate in marine systems.

Sediment–water partition coefficients were calculated using Eqs. (4) and (5) [58]:

Kd=Cs/Cw×1000K_{d} = C_{s}/C_{w} \times 1000

Koc=Kd×100/focK_{oc} = K_{d} \times 100/f_{oc}

where C s and C w denote the concentrations (ng/g and ng/L, respectively) of PPCPs in sediment and water; and f oc represents the total organic carbon content (%) in sediment.

For the compounds examined herein, the log K oc values for EPB and PPB were (3.99 ± 0.95) cm³/g and (3.80 ± 0.57) cm³/g, respectively [图6]. Only these two compounds were selected for log K oc calculation because all others were either undetected in water or undetected in sediment, precluding computation of K d or log K oc. Prior studies rarely report sediment–water partition coefficients for EPB and PPB. Comparison with log K oc values predicted using EPIWEB 4.1 (US EPA) based on compound-specific physicochemical properties revealed that the field-measured log K oc values in this study exceeded their respective predicted values [图6a]. As documented for other compounds, sediment–water partition coefficients are influenced by contaminant properties and usage patterns, aqueous chemical characteristics, and sediment properties [59]. In marine environments, salinity is a key environmental factor governing the partitioning behavior of organic compounds between sediment and water; increasing salinity may alter the affinity of organic compounds for sediment [60]. In this study, neither EPB nor PPB exhibited significant correlations between log K oc values and seawater pH, salinity, or conductivity (p > 0.05), underscoring the complexity of organic contaminant partitioning behavior under real-world conditions. Further research is needed to fully elucidate how environmental factors modulate the sediment–water partitioning behavior of PPCPs.

2.5 Integrated ecological risk assessment of PPCPs and risk-based prioritization

Because PPCPs co-occur as mixtures in marine environments, the toxicity of such mixtures—regardless of individual toxic mechanisms—may represent the sum of the toxic effects of all constituent compounds [61]. Accordingly, this study employed 公式（1） to calculate the aggregate risk quotients (RQs) of PPCPs for algae, crustaceans, and fish, thereby assessing the integrated ecological risk posed by PPCP mixtures to aquatic organisms across trophic levels [8, 15, 16, 29]. Results indicate that although the mixture risk to crustaceans exceeds that to algae and fish, the risk to all three taxonomic groups remains low [图7a]. This finding aligns with prior studies conducted in Selangor, Malaysia [62] and a mariculture zone in Tianjin [63], both reporting low ecological risk; it contrasts with findings from the Hetao Irrigation District [64], where medium-to-high risks were reported for most PPCPs and low risk for only a few—potentially attributable to differences in contaminant profiles and selection of toxicity data [15]. Furthermore, mean ecological risk quotients for PPCP mixtures toward algae, crustaceans, and fish were higher at sampling sites in the Bohai Sea than in the Yellow Sea; however, no statistically significant difference was observed between the two seas (p > 0.05, [图7a]).

To prioritize target PPCPs in seawater from the study area [65], this study applied an optimized risk quotient approach [公式（3）] to compute the prioritization index (PI) for each individual PPCP (detection frequency > 50%) relative to its most sensitive species [图7b]. Results show that the PI for all PPCPs with detection frequency > 50% equals zero, classifying them as safe substances. Although the mixture of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs and preservatives exhibited low risk to all three aquatic taxa in this study, metabolites of PPCPs were not investigated in depth. Prior research [66] indicates that hydroxylated ibuprofen metabolites—including 1-hydroxyibuprofen, 2-hydroxyibuprofen, and 1,2-dihydroxyibuprofen—exhibit higher toxicity than the parent compound ibuprofen. Hence, continued monitoring of both PPCPs and their metabolites is warranted to comprehensively assess ecological risk. Moreover, MPB has been overlooked in previous regional studies; thus, it warrants focused attention in future pollution control efforts.

3 Conclusions

This study investigated the spatiotemporal distribution, sediment–water partition coefficients, ecological risk, and priority ranking of 15 commonly used PPCPs—across two classes—in the nearshore waters of Yantai and Weihai on the Shandong Peninsula. Thirteen and five PPCPs were detected in seawater and sediments, respectively, with concentrations ranging from 2.31 to 662.31 ng/L in seawater and from 57.29 to 126.34 ng/g in sediments. Variations in PPCP concentrations reflect not only seasonal consumption patterns but also intrinsic compound properties and environmental conditions. Vertically, no significant differences were observed between surface and bottom water layers across the three seasons; horizontally, seawater PPCP concentrations at Bohai Sea sampling sites exceeded those at Yellow Sea sites in spring and winter, whereas the reverse pattern occurred in summer—likely attributable to differing environmental conditions and shifts in PPCP consumption patterns. Additionally, environmental factors influence sediment–water partition coefficients, thereby affecting the environmental fate of PPCPs. Ecologically, all PPCPs posed low risk to algae, crustaceans, and fish; nine PPCPs exhibited a prioritization index of zero. However, MPB yielded higher risk quotients than other compounds and at other sites across three sampling locations, underscoring the need for targeted investigation and removal strategy development for MPB in this region.
