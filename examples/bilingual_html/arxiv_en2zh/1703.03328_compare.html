Abstract

Results are reported from an improved measurement of νμ→νe transitions by the NOvA experiment. Using an exposure equivalent to 6.05×1020 protons-on-target 33 νe candidates were observed with a background of 8.2±0.8 (syst.). Combined with the latest NOvA νμ disappearance data and external constraints from reactor experiments on sin2⁡2⁢θ13, the hypothesis of inverted mass hierarchy with θ23 in the lower octant is disfavored at greater than 93% C.L. for all values of δC⁢P.

This Letter reports updated results on the rate of νμ→νe transitions in the NOvA experiment ref:novaFAnue and constraints on oscillation parameters from the first combined fit of νe appearance and νμ disappearance data. The measurement, also probed by MINOS  ref:minosnumu and T2K ref:t2knumu experiments, is sensitive to three unknowns in neutrino physics: the octant of θ23 (whether θ23 is less than, equal to, or greater than π/4), the neutrino mass hierarchy, and the amount of CP violation in the lepton sector. At the baseline and neutrino energy range of the NOvA experiment the probability for νμ to oscillate to νe is primarily proportional to the combination sin2⁡θ23⁢sin2⁡2⁢θ13. The disappearance of muon neutrinos is sensitive to the mixing angle θ23 which is relatively weakly constrained to be near-maximal (sin2⁡θ23≈0.5) ref:minosnumu ; ref:t2knumu ; ref:novaFAnumu . Reactor neutrino measurements tightly constrain sin2⁡2⁢θ13 at 0.085±0.005 ref:dayabay ; ref:reno ; ref:doublechooz . The coherent forward scattering of the neutrino beam with electrons in the Earth enhances the electron neutrino appearance probability in the case of normal mass hierarchy (NH), where Δ⁢m322>0, and suppresses it for inverted mass hierarchy (IH), where Δ⁢m322<0. The possible violation of CP symmetry in the lepton sector is parameterized by δC⁢P. CP-conserving oscillations occur if δC⁢P=0 or π, while νe appearance is enhanced around δC⁢P=3⁢π/2, and suppressed around δC⁢P=π/2. At NOvA’s energy and baseline the impact of these three factors on the νe appearance probability are of similar magnitudes, which can lead to degeneracies between them, particularly when analyzing oscillations in neutrinos alone. For antineutrinos, the mass hierarchy and CP phase have the opposite effect on the oscillation probability, while increasing values of sin2⁡θ23 increase the appearance probabilities for νe and ν¯e alike.

NOvA ref:nova observes neutrinos produced in Fermilab’s NuMI ref:NuMI beamline in two detectors. The Far Detector (FD) is located on the surface, 14.6 mrad off the central beam axis, 810 km from the neutrino parent production source. The Near Detector (ND) is located 100 m underground, 1 km from the source and measures the neutrino beam spectrum before oscillations occur. It is positioned to maximize the overlap between the neutrino energy spectra observed at the two detectors. At these locations, the beam is peaked around 2 GeV with neutrino energies mainly in the 1 to 3 GeV range. According to simulations, the neutrino beam at the ND is predominantly νμ, with 1.8% ν¯μ and 0.7% νe + ν¯e components for neutrino energies between 1 and 3 GeV.

The two functionally equivalent detectors ref:nova ; ref:detector ; ref:novaFAnumu ; ref:novaFAnue are constructed from planes of extruded PVC cells ref:extrusions . The cells have a rectangular cross section measuring 3.9 cm by 6.6 cm and are 15.5 m (3.9 m) long in the FD (ND). Planes alternate the long cell dimension between vertical and horizontal orientations perpendicular to the beam. Each cell is filled with liquid scintillator ref:scint . Light is collected by a loop of wavelength-shifting fiber inside the cell. The fiber ends terminate on a single pixel of an avalanche photodiode (APD) ref:apd . The FD (ND) has a total active mass of 14 kt (193 t). In the fiducial region, the detectors are 62% scintillator by mass.

The data analyzed were collected between February 6, 2014 and May 2, 2016. The exposure is equivalent to 6.05×1020 protons-on-target (POT) collected in the full detector and corresponds to more than double the exposure used in previous results ref:novaFAnumu ; ref:novaFAnue . The fiducial mass for the full detector is 10.3 kt. The average neutrino beam power increased from 250 kW to 560 kW during the data-taking period.

Measuring electron-neutrino appearance requires identification of charged-current (CC) interactions of νe and understanding the various backgrounds that are also selected at the FD. The signature of νe CC interactions in the NOvA detectors is an electromagnetic shower plus any associated hadronic recoil energy. The largest background arises from Neutral Current (NC) interactions of beam neutrinos that produce π0 which decay to photons that mimic the signature of an electron. The intrinsic νe component of the NuMI beam represents an irreducible background to this search. Charged current interactions of νμ with a short muon track and a hadronic shower with some electromagnetic activity comprise a smaller background. Other small backgrounds include cosmic ray induced events, particularly where a photon or a neutron enters from the sides of the detector and charged-current interactions of ντ, which mostly occur above 3 GeV.

For this analysis a new νe⁢CC classifier was developed to select a signal sample with improved purity and efficiency. The Convolutional Visual Network (CVN) cvnpaper is a convolutional neural network and was designed using deep learning techniques from the field of computer vision szegedy2014googlenet ; hinton1986 . Recorded hits in the detectors are formed into clusters by grouping hits in time and space to isolate individual interactions ref:michaelthesis ; ref:dbscan . The CVN classifier takes the hits from these clusters, without any further reconstruction, as input and applies a series of trained linear operations to extract complex, abstract classifying features from the image. A multilayer perceptron ref:mlp1 ; ref:mlp2 at the end of the network uses these features to create the classifier output. Training is conducted using a mixture of simulated FD νμ⁢CC, νe⁢CC, ντ⁢CC, and NC events as well as a sample of FD cosmic data.

The NOvA simulation chain uses FLUKA ref:fluka1 , GEANT4 ref:geant1 , FLUGG ref:flugg , GENIE ref:genie and a custom detector simulation ref:sim to model neutrino production in the beamline and subsequent interaction in the detector. Neutrino scattering off substructure in the nucleus is added to the simulation using an empirical model of multinucleon excitations and long range correlations ref:mec1 ; ref:mec2 ; ref:mec3 ; ref:mec4 . The implementation of this model in the NOvA simulation is tuned to match an observed excess of events in data over simulation in bins of reconstructed three-momentum transfer ref:numuSA . Additionally, the rate of non-resonant single pion production in charged-current interactions is effectively reduced by 50%, motivated by a recent reanalysis of νμ-deuterium pion-production data ref:nonres1pi1 ; ref:nonres1pi2 .

For the purpose of energy reconstruction and event containment, the event cluster is further reconstructed to determine particle paths. A Hough transform is applied to the cluster to identify global features, characterized as Hough linesref:hough . The intersections of these lines seed an algorithm to produce a three dimensional vertex for the cluster ref:earms . In both the horizontal and vertical detector views hits are grouped into prongs radiating from the vertex ref:fuzzyk ; ref:evanthesis . Prongs are then matched between the views based on energy deposition characteristics.

The energy responses of the detectors are calibrated using minimum ionizing energy deposits in a region 1 to 2 meters from the end of tracks corresponding to stopping cosmic ray muons. To reconstruct the electron neutrino candidate energy, the prong with the most calorimetric energy is assumed to be an electromagnetic shower caused by the outgoing electron. The remaining energy deposits in the event are attributed to the hadronic recoil system. The reconstructed νe energy is taken as a quadratic function of the electromagnetic and hadronic calorimetric responses. The function is a parameterization of the simulated true electron neutrino energy in relation to these quantities, and yields an energy resolution of ∼7% in both detectors.

To suppress the cosmic ray induced background in the FD, selected events are required to be in a 12 μs window centered on the 10 μs beam spill. A large fraction of cosmic events deposit energy close to the detector edges and are removed due to containment requirements. Requiring a small reconstructed transverse momentum fraction with respect to the beam direction rejects cosmic events with angles too steep to be consistent with a NuMI beam event. The cosmic background rejection criteria are tuned using neutrino beam simulation and a large sample of cosmic data recorded asynchronously with the neutrino beam.

The maximum of the νe appearance signal is expected just below the peak neutrino energy at NOvA. Restricting the energy range of selected events to 1-3 GeV removes a large fraction of the NC and cosmic backgrounds which are predominately of lower reconstructed energy, and intrinsic νe⁢CC events which dominate at higher energies. We similarly constrain the length of the longest track and number of hits in an event to remove clear muon tracks or poorly reconstructed events. Other than containment requirements, the νe CC selection criteria in the ND are very similar to those in the FD.

The selection criteria are chosen to maximize the figure of merit defined as SS+B, where S and B are the number of signal and background events, respectively. The final νe selection criteria select contained appearance signal with 73.5% efficiency and 75.5% purity, representing a gain in sensitivity of 30% compared to the νe classifiers used in the previously reported results ref:novaFAnue . These criteria also reject 97.6% of the NC and 99.0% of the νμ CC beam backgrounds. The cosmic ray backgrounds are suppressed by seven orders of magnitude, and only 0.53±0.14 cosmic events are estimated to be selected in the final νe appearance sample based on the performance of νe selection criteria on cosmic data. Of the beam backgrounds that pass all νe selection, 91% contain some form of energetic electromagnetic shower. To further improve the statistical power of this analysis, events selected in the FD are split into three νe classifier bins, containing signal νe CC events with low, medium and high purity. The analysis is performed in four energy bins between 1 and 3 GeV for each of the three classifier bins.

The ND has negligible νe appearance signal, and is used to estimate the beam neutrino induced background rates to the appearance measurement. According to simulation, the kinematics of the events that pass the νe CC selection criteria in the ND are representative of and adequately cover those selected in the FD. Figure 1 shows that there is an overall ∼10% excess of data over simulation in the νe CC selected events in the ND. Since the NC, νμ CC and beam νe CC background components are affected differently by oscillations, the total background selected in the ND data is broken down into these components which are then used to estimate the corresponding components in the FD.

Both the νμ and intrinsic νe components of the beam peak arise primarily from pions decaying through the process (π+→μ++νμ), as well as the subsequent muon decay (μ+→e++ν¯μ+νe). At higher energies they originate from kaon decays. The pion and kaon hadron yields can be derived from the low and high-energy νμ⁢CC rate in the ND data and are used to correct the νe CC rate in the simulation. Pion yields are adjusted in bins of transverse and longitudinal pion momentum, while the kaon yield is simply scaled. From this method, it is inferred that the kaon yield is higher by 17% and the pion yield lower by 3% than predicted by the simulation. This results in an overall 1% increase in the estimated intrinsic νe⁢CC background rate in the 1 to 3  GeV range in the ND.

Some of the νμ⁢CC interactions that are a background to the νe⁢CC selection have a muon hidden in the shower associated with the hadronic recoil. In these events, the time-delayed electron from muon decay (Michel electron) may often be found. The hadronic recoil system also produces this signature due to the presence of charged pions that decay to muons. However, on average, νμ⁢CC interactions have one more Michel electron than νe⁢CC and NC interactions. The νμ⁢CC and NC background components are varied in each bin of energy and νe classifier to obtain the best match to the distribution of the number of Michel electron candidates in data. The intrinsic νe⁢CC background component is held fixed at the value obtained from the pion and kaon yield analysis. This method leads to an integrated increase of 17.7% and 10.4% in the νμ⁢CC and NC background rates relative to those predicted by the ND simulation. These corrections derived from the ND data account for the 10% discrepancy with simulation and are applied to the background spectra in the FD simulation in the analysis bins. The spectra are then weighted by the appropriate 3-flavor oscillation probability to obtain the final estimates of the beam backgrounds in the FD. After applying these data-driven constraints, the predicted background composition in the FD for this analysis is 45.3% NC, 38% intrinsic νe CC, 8.4% νμ CC, 1.8% ντ⁢CC, and 6.5% cosmic events.

The νe appearance signal expected in the FD is also constrained by the observed neutrino beam spectrum in the ND. A sample of νμ candidates are selected in the ND data using the latest νμ selection criteria as described in ref:numuSA , and the underlying true energy spectrum is derived from a reconstructed to true energy migration matrix. The spectrum of true νe⁢CC signal events selected in the FD simulation is corrected by the ratio of the νμ⁢CC true energy spectrum derived from ND data to the simulated νμ⁢CC spectrum. The adjusted FD signal spectrum is weighted by the νe appearance probability and mapped back to the reconstructed energy spectrum for the final estimate of νe appearance signal. This extrapolation is carried out for the energy spectra in all three νe classifier bins. Figure 2 shows the variation in the number of FD events predicted as a function of the assumed oscillation parameters.

The ND data are also used to verify the simulated νe CC selection efficiency. For events that pass the νμ CC selection criteria in ND data and simulation, the energy deposits along the reconstructed track of the candidate muon are removed ref:kanikathesis . An electron with the same energy and direction is simulated in its place to construct νe⁢CC-like interactions in both data and simulation. The event is reconstructed again with the electron shower embedded in it and the νe selection cuts are applied. The efficiency of the νe CC selection criteria in the ND between data and simulation for identifying neutrino events with inserted electrons matches to within 1%.

Systematic uncertainties are evaluated by reweighting or generating new simulated event samples modified to account for each uncertainty in the ND and FD. The full analysis, including background component estimation in the ND data and extrapolation to FD, is performed with these systematically shifted simulation samples to predict the altered signal and background spectra at the FD. Calibration and normalization are the leading sources of systematic uncertainty for background and signal, respectively. Other sources of systematic uncertainty considered include neutrino flux, modeling of neutrino interactions and detector response. The overall effect of the uncertainties summed in quadrature on the total event count is 5.0% (10.5%) on the signal (background). The statistical uncertainties of 20.1% (34.9%) on the signal (background) therefore dominate.

To extract oscillation parameters, the νe CC energy spectrum in bins of event classifier is fit simultaneously with the FD νμ CC energy spectrum ref:numuSA . The NOvA νμ disappearance result constrains sin2⁡θ23 around degenerate best fit points of 0.404 and 0.624. The likelihood between the observed spectra and the Poisson expectation in each bin is computed as a function of the oscillation parameters |Δ⁢m322|, θ23, θ13, δC⁢P, and the mass hierarchy. Each source of systematic uncertainty is incorporated into the fit as a nuisance parameter, which varies the predicted FD spectrum according to the shifts determined from systematically shifted samples. Where systematic uncertainties are common between the two data sets, the nuisance parameters associated with the effect are correlated appropriately. Gaussian penalty terms are applied to represent the estimates of the 1⁢σ ranges of these parameters, and the knowledge of sin2⁡2⁢θ13=0.085±0.005 from reactor experiments ref:PDG .

The likelihood surface is profiled over the parameters |Δ⁢m322| and θ13 while the solar parameters Δ⁢m212 and θ12 are held fixed. Figure 5 shows the significance at which values of δC⁢P are disfavored for each hierarchy and octant combination. The value of sin2⁡θ23 is profiled within the specified octant. There are two degenerate best fit points, both in the normal hierarchy, sin2⁡θ23=0.404, δC⁢P=1.48⁢π and sin2⁡θ23=0.623, δC⁢P=0.74⁢π. The inverted hierarchy predicts fewer events than are observed for all values of δC⁢P and both octants. The best-fit point in the inverted hierarchy occurs near δC⁢P=3⁢π/2 and is 0.46 σ from the global best-fit points. The inverted mass hierarchy in the lower octant is disfavored at greater than 93% C.L. for all values of δC⁢P, and excluded at greater than 3⁢σ significance outside the range 0.97⁢π<δC⁢P<1.94⁢π. The T2K collaboration has recently published results based on their observation of νμ (ν¯μ) disappearance and νe (ν¯e) appearance  ref:t2k2017 . While their data favor a near-maximal value of θ23, they disfavor CP conservation at 90% C.L., with a weak preference for normal mass hierarchy. These observations are broadly consistent with the NOvA result.

To conclude, in the first combined fit of the NOvA νe appearance and νμ disappearance data, the inverted mass hierarchy with θ23 in the lower octant is disfavored at greater than 93% C.L. for all values of δC⁢P. Future data-taking in antineutrino mode, where the impact of the mass hierarchy and CP phase are reversed with respect to their effect on neutrinos, will help resolve the remaining degeneracies in the parameters.

Abstract

本文报告了NOvA实验对νμ→νe跃迁的改进测量结果。 该测量所用数据对应于6.05×1020 质子打靶量，共观测到33个νe候选事例，背景为 8.2±0.8（系统误差）。 结合最新的NOvA νμ消失数据以及反应堆实验对sin2⁡2⁢θ13的外部约束，反质量序（IH）且θ23位于低象限的假设在所有δC⁢P取值下均被排除，置信水平高于93%。

本快报报道了NOvA实验 ref:novaFAnue 中νμ→νe跃迁率的最新结果，以及首次对νe出现与νμ消失数据进行联合拟合所得的振荡参数约束。 该测量亦由MINOS ref:minosnumu 和T2K ref:t2knumu 实验开展，其对中微子物理中的三个未知量高度敏感：θ23的象限（即θ23小于、等于还是大于π/4）、中微子质量序，以及轻子扇区中CP破坏的程度。 在NOvA实验的基线长度与中微子能量范围内，νμ振荡为νe的概率主要正比于组合量sin2⁡θ23⁢sin2⁡2⁢θ13。 μ子中微子的消失对混合角θ23敏感，而该角目前仅被较弱地约束为近最大混合（sin2⁡θ23≈0.5） ref:minosnumu ; ref:t2knumu ; ref:novaFAnumu 。 反应堆中微子测量在0.085±0.005处对sin2⁡2⁢θ13给出了严格约束 ref:dayabay ; ref:reno ; ref:doublechooz 。 中微子束流与地球内电子的相干前向散射，在正常质量序（NH，即Δ⁢m322>0）情形下增强电子中微子出现概率，而在反质量序（IH，即Δ⁢m322<0）情形下则抑制该概率。 轻子扇区中CP对称性可能的破缺由δC⁢P参数化。 当δC⁢P=0或π时，发生CP守恒振荡；而νe出现概率在δC⁢P=3⁢π/2附近增强，在δC⁢P=π/2附近受到抑制。 在NOvA的能量与基线条件下，上述三类因素对νe出现概率的影响幅度相近，由此可能导致它们之间出现简并，尤其在仅分析中微子振荡时更为显著。 对于反中微子，质量序与CP相位对振荡概率的影响与中微子情形相反；而sin2⁡θ23增大时，νe与ν¯e的出现概率均随之升高。

NOvA ref:nova 利用费米实验室NuMI ref:NuMI 束流线产生的中微子，在两台探测器中开展观测。 远端探测器（FD）位于地表，偏离束流中心轴14.6 mrad，距中微子母粒子产生源810 km。 近端探测器（ND）位于地下100 m深处，距源1 km，用于在振荡发生前测量中微子束流能谱。 其位置经优化设计，以最大化两台探测器所观测中微子能谱之间的重叠度。 在上述位置，束流能谱峰值约在2 GeV，中微子能量主要集中于1–3 GeV区间。 根据模拟结果，在1–3 GeV中微子能量范围内，ND处的中微子束流以νμ为主，ν¯μ成分占1.8%，νe + ν¯e成分合计占0.7%。

两台功能等效的探测器 ref:nova ; ref:detector ; ref:novaFAnumu ; ref:novaFAnue 由挤出式聚氯乙烯（PVC）单元阵列构成 ref:extrusions 。 各单元呈矩形截面，尺寸为3.9 cm × 6.6 cm；在FD（ND）中长度分别为15.5 m（3.9 m）。 各层单元沿垂直于束流方向交替排布，长边分别取竖直与水平朝向。 每个单元内充填液体闪烁体 ref:scint 。 单元内部嵌入波长移位光纤环以收集光信号。 光纤末端耦合至单像素雪崩光电二极管（APD） ref:apd 。 FD（ND）的总活性质量为14 kt（193 t）。 在符合体积（fiducial region）内，探测器质量的62%为闪烁体。

本分析所用数据采集于2014年2月6日至2016年5月2日。 数据对应于全探测器接收的6.05×1020质子打靶量（POT），曝光量超过此前结果 ref:novaFAnumu ; ref:novaFAnue 所用数据的两倍以上。 全探测器的符合质量为10.3 kt。 数据采集期间，平均中微子束流功率由250 kW提升至560 kW。

电子中微子出现的测量需识别νe的带电流（CC）相互作用，并厘清在FD中被同时选入的各类背景过程。 在NOvA探测器中，νe CC相互作用的特征信号为电磁簇射叠加任何伴随的强子反冲能量。 最主要的背景源于束流中微子的中性流（NC）相互作用，其产生的π0衰变为光子，从而模拟电子信号。 NuMI束流中固有的νe成分构成该搜寻中不可消除的本底。 νμ的带电流相互作用若伴随短程μ子径迹及含一定电磁活性的强子簇射，则构成较小的背景。 其他次要背景包括宇宙线诱发事例（尤其当光子或中子从探测器侧壁入射时），以及ντ的带电流相互作用（主要发生在3 GeV以上能区）。

本分析开发了一种新型νe⁢CC分类器，以提升信号样本的纯度与效率。 卷积视觉网络（CVN） cvnpaper 是一种卷积神经网络，采用计算机视觉领域的深度学习技术设计 szegedy2014googlenet ; hinton1986 。 探测器记录的击中信号（hits）通过时空聚类方法分组，以分离单个相互作用事例 ref:michaelthesis ; ref:dbscan 。 CVN分类器直接以这些聚类的击中信号为输入（无需进一步重建），通过一系列经训练的线性变换，从图像中提取复杂、抽象的判别特征。 网络末端采用多层感知机（MLP） ref:mlp1 ; ref:mlp2 ，利用上述特征生成最终分类输出。 训练过程使用模拟的FD νμ⁢CC、νe⁢CC、ντ⁢CC及NC事例混合样本，以及一组FD宇宙线实测数据。

NOvA模拟链采用FLUKA ref:fluka1 、GEANT4 ref:geant1 、FLUGG ref:flugg 、GENIE ref:genie 及定制化探测器模拟程序 ref:sim ，对束流线中中微子产生及其在探测器内的后续相互作用进行建模。 核内子结构的中微子散射效应，通过经验性的多核子激发与长程关联模型引入模拟 ref:mec1 ; ref:mec2 ; ref:mec3 ; ref:mec4 。 该模型在NOvA模拟中的实现经过调谐，以匹配数据与模拟在重构三维动量转移分布中观测到的事例超出 ref:numuSA 。 此外，受近期对νμ-氘核介子产生数据的再分析启发 ref:nonres1pi1 ; ref:nonres1pi2 ，带电流相互作用中非共振单π介子产生率被有效降低50%。

为实现能量重建与事例包含性判定，需对事例聚类作进一步重建以确定粒子径迹。 对聚类施加霍夫变换（Hough transform），以识别全局特征（表征为霍夫直线） ref:hough 。 这些直线的交点作为种子，启动算法以生成该聚类的三维顶点 ref:earms 。 在探测器的水平与竖直视图中，击中信号均按辐射状分组为若干“支”（prongs），其共同起源于该顶点 ref:fuzzyk ; ref:evanthesis 。 随后依据能量沉积特性，在两个视图间对支进行匹配。

探测器的能量响应利用停止型宇宙线μ子径迹末端1–2米区域内最小电离能量沉积进行校准。 为重建电子中微子候选事例能量，将能量沉积最大的支判定为由末态电子引发的电磁簇射。 事例中其余能量沉积归因于强子反冲系统。 重建的νe能量取为电磁与强子量能响应的二次函数。 该函数是对模拟中真实电子中微子能量与上述两量关系的经验参数化，所得能量分辨率为∼7%，在两台探测器中一致。

为压制FD中的宇宙线背景，所选事例须落入以10 μs束流脉冲为中心、宽度为12 μs的时间窗内。 大量宇宙线事例的能量沉积靠近探测器边缘，因而被包含性要求剔除。 要求事例的横向动量分量相对于束流方向较小，可排除那些入射角过陡、无法与NuMI束流事例一致的宇宙线事件。 宇宙线背景抑制准则的调谐，基于中微子束流模拟及大量与中微子束流异步采集的宇宙线实测数据。

νₑ外观信号的最大值预期出现在NOvA峰值中微子能量略下方。 将所选事例的能量范围限定在1–3 GeV，可剔除大量以较低重建能量为主的中性流（NC）及宇宙线本底，以及在较高能量处占主导地位的本征νₑ带电流传（CC）事例。 类似地，我们对事例中最长径迹长度及 hits 数量施加约束，以剔除明显的μ子径迹或重建质量较差的事例。 除包含性要求外，近探测器（ND）中νₑ CC选择准则与远探测器（FD）中的高度相似。

选择准则的设计目标是最大化图优度（figure of merit），其定义为SS+B，其中S和B分别表示信号事例数与背景事例数。 最终νₑ外观选择准则选取包含型外观信号事例，其效率为73.5%、纯度为75.5%，相较于先前报道结果中所用的νe分类器ref:novaFAnue ，灵敏度提升达30%。 这些准则同时抑制了97.6%的中性流（NC）本底及99.0%的νₘᵤ CC束流本底。 宇宙线本底被压制达七个数量级；基于νe选择准则在宇宙线实测数据上的性能评估，最终νₑ外观样本中预计仅入选0.53±0.14个宇宙线事例。 所有通过νe选择准则的束流本底事例中，91%含有某种形式的高能电磁簇射。 为进一步提升本分析的统计效力，远探测器（FD）中入选的事例按三类νe分类器划分为三个区间，分别对应低、中、高三档纯度的信号νₑ CC事例。 分析在每个分类器区间内，于1–3 GeV能量范围内进一步划分为四个能量区间进行。

近探测器（ND）中νₑ外观信号可忽略不计，故用于估计束流中微子诱发的外观测量本底率。 根据模拟，通过ND中νₑ CC选择准则的事例运动学特征具有代表性，且足以覆盖FD中所选事例的运动学相空间。 图1显示，在ND中经νₑ CC选择准则筛选的事例中，数据整体较模拟高出约∼10%。 由于中性流（NC）、νₘᵤ CC及束流νₑ CC本底组分受振荡影响方式不同，ND数据中所选总本底被分解为上述各组分，并据此估算FD中相应组分。

束流能谱峰位处的νₑ成分及本征νₑ成分主要源于π介子衰变过程（π+→μ++νμ），以及后续μ子衰变（μ+→e++ν¯μ+νe）。 在更高能量区域，则主要源自K介子衰变。 π与K介子产额可由ND数据中低能与高能νμ⁢CC事例率导出，并用于修正模拟中νₑ CC事例率。 π介子产额按横向与纵向动量分箱调整，而K介子产额则仅作整体缩放。 依此方法推断，K介子产额比模拟预测高17%，π介子产额则低3%。 由此导致ND中1–3 GeV能区本征νₑ本底率整体增加1%。

部分作为νₑ选择本底的νₘᵤ相互作用中，μ子被隐藏于强子反冲伴随的簇射内。 此类事例中常可观测到时间延迟的μ子衰变电子（Michel电子）。 强子反冲系统亦因带电π介子衰变为μ子而产生该特征信号。 然而，平均而言，νₘᵤ相互作用比νₑ及中性流（NC）相互作用多含一个Michel电子。 νₘᵤ CC与NC本底组分在各能量区间及νe分类器区间内被独立调节，以最优匹配数据中Michel电子候选体数目分布。 本征νₑ本底组分则固定为π与K介子产额分析所得值。 该方法导致νₘᵤ CC与NC本底率相对于ND模拟预测值分别整体增加17.7%与10.4%。 这些由ND数据导出的修正项解释了与模拟之间10%的差异，并应用于FD模拟本底谱在分析区间内的校正。 随后，谱形按适当的三味中微子振荡概率加权，获得FD中束流本底的最终估计。 应用上述数据驱动约束后，本分析中FD预测本底组成为：45.3%中性流（NC）、38%本征νₑ CC、8.4% νₘᵤ CC、1.8% ντ⁢CC、6.5%宇宙线事例。

FD中预期的νₑ外观信号亦受ND观测中微子束流能谱约束。 依据ref:numuSA 所述最新νₑ选择准则，在ND数据中选取νₑ候选事例样本，并利用重建能–真能迁移矩阵反演其真实能谱。 FD模拟中选出的真实νₑ信号事例能谱，按ND数据导出的真实νₑ能谱与模拟νₑ能谱之比进行修正。 经修正的FD信号能谱再按νₑ外观振荡概率加权，并映射回重建能谱，从而获得νₑ外观信号的最终估计。 该外推过程在全部三类νe分类器区间内分别执行。 图2展示了FD事件数预测值随假设振荡参数变化的情况。

ND数据亦用于验证模拟中νₑ CC选择效率。 对ND数据与模拟中通过νₑ CC选择准则的事例，移除候选μ子重建径迹沿线的能量沉积ref:kanikathesis 。 在其原位模拟注入具有相同能量与方向的电子，从而在数据与模拟中构建类νₑ相互作用。 将嵌入电子簇射后的事件重新重建，并施加νₑ选择截断条件。 ND中νₑ CC选择准则在数据与模拟间对含注入电子的中微子事例识别效率一致，偏差在1%以内。

系统误差通过重加权或生成经系统性修正的新模拟事例样本进行评估，修正涵盖ND与FD中各项不确定性来源。 完整分析流程（包括ND数据中本底组分估计及向FD外推）均使用这些系统性偏移的模拟样本执行，以预测FD处信号与本底谱的改变。 校准与归一化分别为本底与信号最主要的系统误差来源。 其他考虑的系统误差来源包括中微子束流、中微子相互作用建模及探测器响应。 各项不确定性按平方和开方合成后，对总事例数的影响为：信号5.0%，本底10.5%。 因此，信号（20.1%）与本底（34.9%）的统计误差占主导地位。

在事例选择准则与分析流程最终确定后，对FD数据的检查发现33个νₑ候选事例，其中背景事例预期为8.2±0.8（系统误差）。背景事例数按最佳拟合振荡参数sin2⁡θ23=0.40,sin2⁡2⁢θ13=0.085,Δ⁢m322=2.67×10−3⁢eV2⁢and⁢δCP=1.48⁢π计算得出。物质密度采用CRUST2.0模型ref:crust ，按NuMI束流在NOvA基线810 km路径上穿越地壳的平均深度计算，为2.84 g/cm³。 图3展示了事例分布与最佳拟合点预期值在分类器变量及重建中微子能量维度上的对比。

为提取振荡参数，将按事例分类器分箱的νe带电流（CC）能量谱与远探测器（FD）的νμ带电流（CC）能量谱同时进行拟合 ref:numuSA 。 NOvA的νμ消失测量结果将sin2⁡θ23约束在两个简并的最佳拟合点附近：0.404与0.624。 似然函数定义为各能量箱中观测谱与泊松期望值之间的似然，其为振荡参数|Δ⁢m322|、θ23、θ13、δC⁢P及质量序（mass hierarchy）的函数。 每类系统误差均以“干扰参数”（nuisance parameter）形式纳入拟合；该参数依据系统性偏移样本所确定的偏移量，调节远探测器预期谱。 当两类数据集共享某类系统误差时，对应干扰参数之间按物理关联性作适当关联。 引入高斯惩罚项，以表征这些参数的1⁢σ取值范围估计，并体现反应堆实验 ref:PDG 所给出的sin2⁡2⁢θ13=0.085±0.005先验知识。

图5展示了在不同质量序与象限组合下，δC⁢P各取值被排除的显著性。 sin2⁡θ23在指定象限内作轮廓化处理。 存在两个简并的最佳拟合点，均位于正常质量序下：sin2⁡θ23=0.404、δC⁢P=1.48⁢π 与 sin2⁡θ23=0.623、δC⁢P=0.74⁢π。 反常质量序在所有δC⁢P取值及两种象限下均预言事例数少于实际观测值。 反常质量序下的最佳拟合点出现在δC⁢P=3⁢π/2附近，距全局最佳拟合点为0.46 σ。 反常质量序且处于下象限的情形，在全部δC⁢P取值下均被排除至高于93%置信水平（C.L.），并在0.97⁢π<δC⁢P<1.94⁢π范围之外被排除至高于3⁢σ显著性水平。 T2K合作组近期发表了基于其观测到的νμ（ν¯μ）消失与νe（ν¯e）出现的结果 ref:t2k2017 。 尽管其数据倾向于θ23接近最大混合角，但以90%置信水平排除了CP守恒，并微弱偏好正常质量序。 这些观测结果与NOvA结果总体一致。

综上所述，在NOvA实验对νe出现与νμ消失数据的首次联合拟合中，反常质量序且θ23位于下八分象限的情形，在全部δC⁢P取值下均被排除至高于93%置信水平（C.L.）。 未来在反中微子模式下的数据采集工作将有助于厘清参数中尚存的简并性——在此模式下，质量序与CP相位的影响相对于中微子模式发生反转。
